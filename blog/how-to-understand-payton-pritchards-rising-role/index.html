The Rise of Payton Pritchard: From Underdog to Key Contributor

A Journey Beyond the Draft

Early Career: The College Star

Pritchard’s success at Oregon provided a blueprint for his NBA potential. Averaging 18.4 points per game in 2019–2020, he became a cornerstone of the Ducks’ program, known for his elite three-point shooting (40.2% in 2019–2020) and ability to carry games in critical moments. His 2020 NCAA Tournament run, highlighted by 31 points against Texas Tech, solidified his reputation as a clutch performer. Yet, his college profile wasn't simply about numbers—it was a blend of scoring efficiency, defensive versatility, and leadership.

Translating that success to the NBA presented a different set of hurdles. Pritchard’s 6’2” frame and 225-pound build offered athleticism but limited his ability to guard perimeter threats. His first season with the Boston Celtics in 2021–22 was a mix of promise and struggle. While he averaged 14.1 points per game, his 41.8% shooting from three-point range stood out. But questions arose about his defensive contributions and decision-making under pressure.

The Draft: A Turning Point

Pritchard’s 2021 NBA Draft selection reflected his college profile and the Celtics’ need for a shooter. Analysts like ESPN’s Jonathan Tjarks noted, “Pritchard’s shooting and clutch instincts made him a top-25 pick, but his NBA-ready skills were still a work in progress.” The Celtics’ decision to take him over players like Jalen Duren or Jalen Green was a gamble on his potential to adapt.

The First Seasons: Finding His Niche

Pritchard’s early NBA career was marked by a steep learning curve. The physicality of the league, the faster pace, and the need to defend elite shooters tested his readiness. Yet, moments like his 31-point performance against the Brooklyn Nets in December 2021 signaled his ability to thrive in high-stakes games. By the 2022–23 season, he had established himself as a reliable scorer, averaging 15.3 points per game while improving his efficiency and understanding of team dynamics.

Evolving Role: From Scorer to Playmaker

The Shift in Responsibilities

Pritchard’s role has evolved beyond a pure scorer to a more versatile contributor. His usage rate increased from 20.8% in 2021–22 to 24.1% in 2022–23, reflecting his growing importance to the Celtics’ offense. While scoring remains a key asset—his 38.3% three-point shooting in 2022–23 ranks among the league’s top 10—his ability to create opportunities for teammates has become a defining trait.

The Athletic’s Nick Friedell highlights this shift: “Pritchard’s role has expanded beyond just shooting. He’s learning to read defenses, make smarter passes, and take on more responsibility in pick-and-roll situations.” This evolution mirrors the trend of modern NBA players who blend scoring with playmaking—a trend Pritchard is increasingly embracing.

Adapting to Team Systems

Pritchard’s playing style has also adapted to fit different coaching schemes. Under coach Joe Mazzulla, the Celtics emphasized ball movement and spacing, allowing Pritchard to thrive as a shooter and off-ball threat. In contrast, during the 2023 playoffs, his role shifted to a more traditional scoring guard, with the team relying on him to carry the load in high-leverage moments.

Consider his 2023 playoff performance as a case study. In Game 4 against the Knicks, Pritchard scored 28 points on 11-of-18 shooting, including 6-of-10 from three, while also contributing 6 assists. This balance of scoring and playmaking underscored his value as a versatile threat.

The Mental Game: Growth Beyond the Court

Pritchard’s growth extends beyond the hardwood. His focus on film study, decision-making, and handling pressure has been critical to his development. Joe Mazzulla, now head coach, once observed, “Pritchard’s ability to adjust to different systems and stay composed under pressure is what sets him apart.”

This mental maturity is evident in his handling of criticism. After a slow start to the 2022–23 season, Pritchard leaned into his role as a shooter rather than a playmaker, a strategic shift that helped him regain confidence. His approach to the game has become more calculated, with a focus on maximizing strengths while minimizing weaknesses.

The Impact of Payton Pritchard: Beyond the Box Score

Clutch Performances That Define His Legacy

Pritchard’s ability to deliver in high-stakes games has become a hallmark of his career. These moments aren't just about scoring—they’re about leadership, composure, and the ability to rise to the occasion.

Team Chemistry and Leadership
