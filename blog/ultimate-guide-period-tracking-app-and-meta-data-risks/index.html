Understanding Metadata Risks in Period Tracking

What Is Metadata in the Context of Health Apps?

Metadata refers to data about data – your cycle data – such as timestamps, location, and device information. While not directly revealing sensitive health details, metadata risks can expose users to targeted advertising or data leaks.

  • Examples of metadata:
    • Time of last menstrual cycle (TLC)
    • Location history
    • Device identifiers
  • Metadata risks are often overlooked in privacy discussions.

Period tracking apps collect your cycle data to provide insights, but this data can be exploited if improperly secured. Location data, for instance, could reveal patterns indicating a user's daily routine, potentially leading to identity theft or targeted advertising.
