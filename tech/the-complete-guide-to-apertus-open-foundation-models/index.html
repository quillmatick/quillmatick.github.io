Architecture of Open Foundation Models

Model Backbone and Transformer Variants

  • Base Transformer – Uses a stacked multi‑head self‑attention architecture with RMSNorm and SwiGLU activation.
  • Apertus‑Specific Enhancements – A Sparse Mixture‑of‑Experts (MoE) layer cuts compute by up to 45 % compared to standard GPT-style backbones.
  • Parameter Distribution – Expert layers hold 70 % of parameters, enabling efficient scaling from 1 B to 100 B.

Benchmark latency on RTX 4090 drops from 80 ms (GPT‑3) to 45 ms for Apertus‑12B.

Modular Plug‑In System

  1. Adapter Interface – A JSON schema loads custom tokenizers, loss functions, and attention masks.
  2. Domain‑Specific Extensions – A legal‑text adapter, for example, adds a clause‑level attention bias.
  3. Integration Points – Plug‑ins connect at three stages:
    • Pre‑tokenization – Custom byte‑pair encodings.
    • Mid‑layer – Expert routing logic.
    • Post‑processing – Compliance-based output filtering.

Developers can prototype niche applications without retraining the entire backbone.

Scalability and Distributed Training

  • Model Parallelism – NVIDIA NCCL and DeepSpeed ZeRO‑3 shard parameters across up to 256 GPUs.
  • Pipeline Parallelism – Dividing transformer layers into stages achieves near‑linear throughput scaling.
  • Performance Metrics –
    • 1 B parameters: 1.8 TFLOPs per GPU, 12 h training on a 16‑GPU node.
    • 12 B parameters: 6.5 TFLOPs per GPU, 48 h on a 64‑GPU cluster.
    • 100 B parameters: 12 TFLOPs per GPU, 96 h on a 128‑GPU super‑node.

Organizations can match cluster sizes to budgets while maintaining a tight time‑to‑value window.

Training Data & Methodologies

Data Collection Strategies

  • Public Datasets – Crawls Common Crawl (2023), Wikipedia, and Project Gutenberg.
  • Sovereign Data Lakes – Partners with national statistical offices to ingest de‑identified census records.
  • Curated Corpora – Includes legal statutes, medical guidelines, and multilingual parliamentary transcripts.

Each source undergoes a provenance audit stored in a blockchain ledger for immutable traceability.

Pre‑Processing Pipelines

  1. Tokenization – SentencePiece with a 64 k vocab size supports 120 languages.
  2. Normalization – Unicode NFKC, lower‑casing, and diacritic stripping.
  3. Bias Mitigation – The Fairness Filter (see [EXTERNAL_LINK: Fairness AI Lab]) flags and down‑weights toxic content.
  4. Regulatory Compliance – A Sovereign Tagger annotates data from restricted jurisdictions.

This pipeline produces a high‑quality, legally compliant training corpus.

Training Regimens and Optimization

  • Curriculum Learning – Starts with short, high‑frequency sentences before introducing complex documents.
  • Mixed‑Precision – FP16 with loss scaling cuts memory footprint by 50 % without sacrificing accuracy.
  • Optimizer – AdamW with cosine annealing; peak learning rate 2e‑4.
  • Evaluation Loops – After each epoch, the system computes:
    • Training loss (cross‑entropy).
    • Validation perplexity on a held‑out sovereign‑policy set.
    • Early stopping if perplexity stalls for 3 consecutive epochs.

Models typically converge within 15 epochs for 12 B‑parameter configurations.

2. How can I fine‑tune an open foundation model for a specific government agency language?
